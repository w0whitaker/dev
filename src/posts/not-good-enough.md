# I'm not good enough to make things that people will see, including me.

I woke up this morning at 2:30. Sometimes this happens, 
it happened today because my CPAP mask was making lots 
of noise. Also my foot hurt some because I had some very 
expensive injections in it desperately  trying to get 
rid of some plantar fascitis that's been bothering me for 
the last two years.

I got up and did what I usually do, which is also the 
last thing you should do when you can't sleep. I got on 
the computer and promptly broke it because I'm 
overweight. Ha! No, I opened my laptop and started 
reading about [Zettelkastens](https://zettelkasten.de/posts/overview/) that I found by way of Maggie 
Appleton's [site](https://maggieappleton.com/programmatic-notes), which is one of my favorites.

Always one to get excited about new systems or habits, I 
promptly downloaded [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) and 
got to work setting up my own Zettelkasten, by which I 
mean that I spent two hours downloading fonts and 
playing around with fonts and changing colors and themes 
and whatnot. I did not create any notes. Then I got 
tired and went back to bed and here it is 11 hours later 
and I have managed to write one note, but there's a 
familiar feeling of disappointment in myself that isn't 
exactly rational but _very_ palpable.

Finishing things is a constant problem in my life. When 
I was a kid, I built model airplanes but seldom finished 
them. I have all sorts of projects in my 'development' 
folder that remain unfinished. If it sounds like I 
suffer from a kind of perfectionism, I don't. I'm just 
afraid of revealing faults.

So here is a blog post. It doesn't have a point, it 
doesn't make an argument, and it doesn't have a 
narrative arc. I just started writing it and now I'm 
finished and I am going to post it and maybe tomorrow 
I'll write another. 

Nap time!
