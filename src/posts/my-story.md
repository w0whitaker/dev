## What this is for.
To record in a very brief way the events and memories I have about my relationship to computers, and web development specifically.

### Early memories
#### 1. Pac-man
My dad is driving me to a birthday party at an arcade. He is talking about Pac-man, and tells me that he doesn't want me spending all my time at the party playing video games. I am 6-8 years old.
#### 2. Timex Sinclair
I am at a friend's house. They have recently gotten a [Timex/Sinclair](https://www.timexsinclair.com/computers/timex-sinclair-1000/) home computer. We are in the kitchen on an afternoon after school. I am afraid to enter anything because I am afraid I will break their brand-new computer. My friend tells me that there is "no way" I could break it by typing in the wrong thing. I will later learn that this is both true and not true.
##### Postcript.
In what I remember as making me feel somewhat ashamed (although why I couldn't say), this Timex/Sinclair computer is given to my family when my friend's family gets a new computer.
#### 3. Radar Rat Race
On Christmas morning, there is a present for me and my sister. It is a Commodore 64 and a book about programming in BASIC. There are also 3 cartridges, one is a Pac-man knock-off called "Radar Rat Race", and the other two are music related. One is a synthesizer-type of thing, and the other I can't remember anything about except that it was boring. The synthesizer I loved. I thought holding one note while changing the waveform was the coolest thing ever. This maybe where my love of Stereolab-type lock-groove music originated.
We were admonished that the Commodore was for learning about computers and not for playing games, which lasted about as long as you'd expect. For one thing, we didn't have a floppy disk drive until years later so any programs beyond "10 PRINT WHATEVER 20 GOTO 10" were impractical.
#### 4. Line spacing and Paint.
In high school, I was always being assigned papers that had to be so many pages long, single spaced. I discovered that word processors allowed you to set line-spacing to an arbitrary number, such as "1.5" (or "1.25" if I was feeling cautious), and so get around the single spaced requirement. I was very excited, although in retrospect it probably only saved me writing a sentence or two at most. 
Also, I made a bunch of album covers for made-up bands in Paint, none of which I saved.
